About the practice

A welcoming practice rooted in care

I'm the teacher behind Yoga Leaf, and I came to yoga not as a fitness pursuit, but as something I genuinely needed — a way to slow down, breathe, and reconnect with myself. After completing my 200-hour Yoga Teacher Training certification and studying mindfulness-based movement, I opened Yoga Leaf right here in Leith because I wanted my neighbours to have access to thoughtful, accessible yoga without the pressure of a commercial gym environment.

My training draws on Hatha, Yin, and restorative traditions, and I'm a registered member of Yoga Alliance Professionals — the UK's leading yoga professional body. Every class and every one-to-one session is shaped around the person in front of me, not a fixed syllabus.

Whether you've never rolled out a yoga mat in your life, or you're returning after years away, you'll find an honest, unhurried welcome at Yoga Leaf. We're based at 6 Henderson Street, Leith — a short walk from the Water of Leith and easy to reach from across Edinburgh.

What we work with

Areas where yoga

Stress & Overwhelm

When life feels relentless, regular yoga practice creates pockets of genuine calm — teaching your nervous system that it's safe to rest.

Tension & Tight Muscles

Desk work, driving, and daily life accumulate in your body. We gently work through the places you carry it most — hips, shoulders, lower back.

Poor Sleep & Low Energy

Yin and restorative yoga can help regulate your body's rest signals, making it easier to wind down and wake up feeling genuinely restored.

Building Flexibility & Strength

Whether you're starting from scratch or building on an existing practice, we progress at a pace that feels right for your body, not a benchmark.
