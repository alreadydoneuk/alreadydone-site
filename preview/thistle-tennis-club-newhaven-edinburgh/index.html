Who this is for

Three Situations Where We Can Help

If any of these sound familiar, you're in the right place. We work with players across Edinburgh and the Lothians at every stage of the game.

01

The Frustrated Beginner

You've tried hitting a few balls with friends and you enjoy it — but you have no idea what you're actually doing wrong. Sessions feel like luck rather than skill, and you want proper fundamentals so you can enjoy the game properly from the start.

02

The Stalled Club Player

You play regularly at your local club but you've been at the same level for two or three seasons. Your backhand lets you down under pressure, your serve is inconsistent, and you know without help you'll stay stuck exactly where you are.

03

The Junior or Youth Player

You're a parent whose child has shown a real interest in tennis and you want to give them proper coaching rather than letting that enthusiasm fizzle out. You want a coach who keeps young players engaged, challenged, and coming back each week.

What we offer

Coaching Programmes Designed to Deliver Results

Every session is planned with a purpose. No generic drills, no wasted court time. Here's exactly what each programme involves.

One-to-one

Private Lessons

Dedicated one-hour sessions focused entirely on your game. We'll identify the technical issues holding you back and work through them systematically, tracking progress so you can see what's changing.

  • Full technique assessment in session one
  • Individual session plan sent before each lesson
  • Video analysis on request
  • Practice drills to use in your own time
  • Flexible scheduling around your diary

Small groups

Group Coaching Sessions

A cost-effective way to improve in a sociable setting. Groups are kept small — never more than six players — so you still get meaningful feedback and court time in every session.

  • Maximum 6 players per session
  • Ability-matched groups
  • Focus areas rotated across strokes and tactics
  • Ideal for club players wanting structured practice
  • Available weekday evenings and weekends

Juniors

Junior Development Programme

A structured, progressive pathway for players aged 6–16. Sessions are designed to be fun and physically appropriate for each age group, while building the technical foundation they'll need as they improve.

  • Mini tennis through to full-court programme
  • Age and stage appropriate session planning
  • Regular progress updates for parents
  • Preparation for club competitions and LTA events
  • Holiday intensive courses available

Beginners

Adult Beginner Courses

A six-week structured course specifically for adults who are new to tennis or returning after a long break. By the end you'll have the skills and confidence to enjoy a proper game — and know exactly what to work on next.

  • Six consecutive weekly sessions
  • Grip, stance, groundstrokes, and serving covered
  • Scoring and match play introduced in week four
  • Small groups — a relaxed, no-pressure environment
  • Course completion certificate from Thistle Tennis Club

Why it matters who coaches you

Qualifications & Experience

The difference between a good coach and a great one comes down to training, experience, and ongoing development. Here's ours.

  • LTA Licensed Coach Licensed by the Lawn Tennis Association — the governing body for tennis in Great Britain. LTA licensing means your coach has met verified competency standards for coaching adults and juniors, holds current safeguarding qualifications, and is regularly assessed. This is non-negotiable when choosing a coach for yourself or your child.
  • 15+ Years on Court Over 15 years of active coaching experience across club, recreational, and junior development environments in Scotland. This isn't classroom knowledge — it's thousands of hours of reading players, adapting in real time, and knowing which technical fix will make the biggest difference for each individual.
  • DBS Checked Enhanced Disclosure and Barring Service certificate — current and up to date. Mandatory for coaching juniors and a basic standard of trust for any parent looking to book coaching for their child. We take safeguarding seriously, not as a box-ticking exercise but as a genuine responsibility.
  • First Aid Certified Full paediatric and adult first aid certification, renewed every three years. Accidents on court are rare, but when they happen your coach needs to respond correctly and immediately. Players and parents should be confident that appropriate first aid can be administered before emergency services arrive.
  • Edinburgh & Lothians Locally based and actively coaching in the Edinburgh area. Based at Katesmill Road, serving Newhaven, Leith, Portobello, and across the Lothians. We know the local club landscape, which competitions matter for junior development in Scotland, and how the weather affects court availability — because we're here every week.
